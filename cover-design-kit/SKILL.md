# Cover Design Kit

**Core principle:** AI art as ideation, never as shipped pixels. Human illustrator commissioned for every cover. Retained across all 6 books where possible. Budget $400–$1,200 per cover from Reedsy, MiblArt, Damonza's illustrated tier, Covers by Christian, Seventhstar Art, or direct ArtStation/Instagram hire.

See `references/illustrator-brief-template.md`, `references/thumbnail-test-protocol.md`, `references/kdp-cover-specs.md`, `references/ai-fingerprint-rejection.md`.

## Cozy Fantasy Visual Grammar (2024–2026)

**Composition:** Character + place. Small-to-medium figure inside/in-front-of a distinctive location (inn, tea shop, cottage, glade). Three-quarter or back view — inviting, not confrontational. Optional animal familiar.

**Palette:** Warm saturated mid-tones. Greens (sage, forest, emerald), warm golds/ambers, terracotta, cream, dusk purples, rust. Single warm light source (hearth, lantern, window) carrying the image.

**Rendering:** Painterly/illustrated, flat-ish with visible texture. Studio Ghibli storybook energy for adults. NOT photoreal, NOT anime, NOT vector-flat.

**Typography:** Warm serif with hand-drawn character, OR clean display serif with illustrated flourishes. Titles often arched, integrated (carved-in-wood, signage). Author name smaller, clean serif/sans.

**Iconography:** Teacups, spellbooks, keys, herb sprigs, fireflies, steaming mugs, pastries, ink pots.

**Differentiate from:**
- Romantasy — sultry dark, high-contrast jewel, figure in provocative pose
- Epic fantasy — heroic pose, metallic type, battle vista
- Urban fantasy — leather-jacket + cityscape, neon

## Series Branding (invariant across all books in a series)

- Title typography lockup (same face, treatment, position)
- Author name treatment
- Series badge ("Series Name · Book N") same treatment and position
- Palette family (warm core; one accent shifts per book seasonally)
- Same illustrator (single biggest lever)
- Recurring motif visible on every cover

**Per-book variation:** season/time-of-day, focal character, location within the series world, accent color.

**Template reference:** Rebecca Thorne's *Tomes & Tea* series is the cleanest template to study.

## Production Pipeline (4–6 weeks per cover)

- Week 0: Brief intake from EIC → brief doc → EIC approval
- Week 1: Moodboard + AI-ideation internal sketches
- Weeks 1–2: Illustrator brief + 2–3 thumbnail concepts back
- Week 2: Concept selection + CEO sign-off on direction
- Weeks 3–4: Illustration refinement (2 revision rounds budgeted)
- Week 5: Typography + composite in Affinity Designer. Thumbnail test. Iterate.
- Weeks 5–6: Final delivery — ebook JPG, paperback PDF wrap, hardcover PDF if applicable, A+ Content assets, social reveal assets

## Thumbnail Test (every milestone)

See `references/thumbnail-test-protocol.md`. Scale to 150px wide. Title legibility <1 second. Genre read <2 seconds. Series consistency + differentiation. Fail any = back to composite.

## Hard Rules

- No AI-generated final pixels ship to Amazon. Ever. Terminating offense.
- KDP AI-disclosure compliance (Amazon policy, Sept 2023+).
- Retain same illustrator across the series unless explicitly overridden.
- Typography must pass 150px legibility before sign-off.
- Cover-to-content alignment verified by EIC.
- Spine-width recalculated when Formatter/KDP Ops locks page count.

## KPIs

- Thumbnail CTR (via Amazon Ads proxy): cozy fantasy target >0.4%, >0.5% strong
- Cover-to-content alignment (EIC rates 1–5 post-read): target ≥4.5
- Revision rounds: illustrator ≤2, typography ≤2
- Cost per cover: $600–$900 target
- Series consistency rubric: ≥95%
- Zero "looks AI" / "cover misled me" reviews post-launch
