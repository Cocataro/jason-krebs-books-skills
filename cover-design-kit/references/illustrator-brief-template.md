# Illustrator Brief Template

Send to the retained illustrator at Week 1 per cover. EIC approves brief before illustrator engagement.

```
PROJECT: [Series Name], Book N — [Title]
AUTHOR: Jason Krebs (pen persona)
GENRE: Cozy Fantasy
ILLUSTRATOR SERIES CONTRACT: [retained / new]

BOOK LOGLINE (1–2 sentences)
[Fill from manuscript]

SEASON / TIME OF DAY
[e.g. late autumn, golden hour]

MOOD
[3–5 adjectives from the warm/cozy register]

COMPOSITION
- Focal figure(s): [character + pose, three-quarter or back view preferred]
- Setting: [inn / shop / cottage / other]
- Must-include elements: [list 2–4]
- Recurring series motif: [the [Inn] visible in: full / partial / silhouette / sign only]

PALETTE
- Core family: [series palette]
- This book's accent color: [seasonal shift]
- Light source: [single warm source — hearth / lantern / window / firefly]

TYPOGRAPHY LOCKUP (for reference; composited in-house at Week 5)
- Title placement: [top third, arched, banner]
- Author name placement: [bottom, clean]
- Series badge placement: [consistent series position]

REFERENCES (attached)
- Top 20 cozy fantasy Amazon screenshot
- 3–5 comp covers
- Pinterest moodboard link
- Prior Book covers in this series (if applicable)

DELIVERABLES
- Round 1: 2–3 thumbnail concept sketches (pencil or rough color)
- Round 2: Flat color on selected concept
- Round 3: Final render, 300dpi, layered PSD or equivalent, large enough for 2560×1600 ebook + 6×9 paperback wrap

REVISION ALLOWANCE
- 2 rounds on concept
- 2 rounds on color
- Additional rounds billed hourly

TIMELINE
- Concept sketches due: [date]
- Flat color due: [date]
- Final render due: [date]
- Cover lock: [date]

DO NOT
- AI-generated elements in final delivery — ship only hand-illustrated pixels
- Sultry / romantasy poses
- Apocalyptic / heroic staging
- Uncanny-valley faces (prefer three-quarter or back view if face is uncertain)

BUDGET: $[amount] flat rate for all rounds listed above

CONTACT / PAYMENT: [per retainer contract]
```

## Notes

- EIC gates the brief before it goes to the illustrator. Never commission without brief approval.
- Retained illustrator across series is the single biggest quality lever. Protect the relationship.
- If the illustrator missed the brief in Round 1, it's almost always a brief problem — revise the brief, not just the sketch.
