# Blurb Anatomy

Heartland Fantasy is a **vibe genre**. Readers buy the feeling, not the plot. The blurb has to deliver the vibe in the first two lines or the reader scrolls.

See `references/heartland-blurb-template.md` for a working template; `references/keyword-discipline.md` for keyword rules; `references/forbidden-keywords.md` for Amazon TOS blocked terms.

## The 5-Part Structure (high-converting Heartland Fantasy)

Target **150–250 words** above-the-fold. Amazon truncates at ~400 chars desktop / ~200 chars mobile before "Read More." **60%+ of readers never click Read More.** Hook + first half of premise must stand alone.

1. **Hook line** (bold or italic, one sentence). Promise of the vibe or premise-as-question. Specific and warm.
2. **Premise paragraph** (40–70 words). Who, where, what world, what cozy hook. Heartland Fantasy is architectural — establish the shop / inn / cottage.
3. **Stakes paragraph** (40–70 words). Personal, relational, local. **NEVER apocalyptic.** Emily Wilde stakes: "an academic may have made a terrible mistake with the faeries."
4. **Promise / vibe line** (15–30 words). Explicit comfort-read positioning. "Pour a cup of tea and settle in."
5. **CTA + comp titles.** "Perfect for fans of *Legends & Lattes* and *Can't Spell Treason Without Tea*."

## Framework: Bryan Cohen, Softened

Cohen's framework (Character → Conflict → Consequence/Hook) is indie default. Honed on thriller/romance. **For cozy, soften the Conflict beat** — make it a complication, not a threat. A literal Cohen blurb on cozy reads over-urgent.

## Heartland Fantasy Language That Works

- **Sensory comfort:** *steaming, flickering, worn, tucked, hearth, lamplit, honey-warm*
- **Relational promise:** *found family, slow burn, soft magic, second chances*
- **Specificity over adjectives:** "a bakery that sells memories" > "a charming magical bakery"

## What Kills Cozy Blurbs

- Generic fantasy verbs: *must, journey, destiny, ancient evil*
- Telling not showing: "You'll laugh, you'll cry"
- Withholding the hook to "tease" — cozy readers want the promise up front
- Keyword-stuffed subtitle (TOS risk + spammy)

## Title + Subtitle Rules

Subtitle = **one genre phrase + series identifier.** That's it.

- Good: "A Heartland Fantasy Novel (The Crossroads Inn Book 1)"
- Bad: "A Heartland Fantasy Slow Burn Romance Found Family Magical Inn Novel" — Amazon has been actively rejecting keyword-stuffed subtitles since 2022.

Amazon auto-appends series in parens on the product page — let the series field handle numbering.

## Categories (3 slots, 2023 expansion)

Target: rank #1 in a narrow sub-category for the orange #1 Bestseller tag.

Primary Heartland Fantasy paths:
- Kindle eBooks → Literature & Fiction → Genre Fiction → Fantasy → **Cozy**
- Kindle eBooks → Literature & Fiction → Fantasy → **Romantic**
- Kindle eBooks → Literature & Fiction → Women's Fiction → **Fantasy** (if tilts that way)
- Kindle eBooks → Literature & Fiction → **Magical Realism** (soft literary tilt)

Hidden-category support-ticket path: ~50% hit rate post-2023. Try per book, don't rely on.

Categories consistent across all books in a series unless genre genuinely shifts.

## A+ Content (design once, template across series)

- **Title banner** — cover + tagline
- **Series banner** — all book covers laid out (critical for read-through on Book 2+)
- **Comparison table** — "Which [Series] book should I start with?" (reduces Book 2+ decision friction)
- **Quote callouts** — ARC reviewer / editorial review pulls
- **Author bio banner** — photo + 2-line bio, humanizes pen persona

## Refresh Cadence

- **Launch +30:** first refresh based on 30-day conversion signal (Ads CTR, rank trajectory)
- **Every new release:** update every prior book's blurb to reference the new one
- **Quarterly:** full backlist keyword + category + comp-title refresh
- **Annual:** author bio refresh

## Hard Rules

- **Read the book.** Non-negotiable.
- **No spoilers** — cozy readers forgive thin plots; never forgive spoilers.
- **Mobile-first formatting** — 70% of readers are phone/tablet.
- EIC approves voice + alignment. CEO Jason approves brand-level (title, subtitle, series name, author bio).
- No trademarked or forbidden keywords (see references/).
