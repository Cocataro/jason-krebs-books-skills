# Heartland Fantasy Blurb Template

Drop into the KDP description field after validating HTML via Kindlepreneur Description Generator. Bold the hook. Italicize comp titles. Single blank lines between paragraphs.

```html
<b><i>[HOOK LINE — one sentence, premise-as-promise or premise-as-question, 12–22 words]</i></b>

[PREMISE — 40–70 words. Who, where, what world. Establish the architectural heart (inn/shop/cottage). Warm sensory anchor. Specificity over adjectives.]

[STAKES — 40–70 words. Personal / relational / local. Never apocalyptic. What's at risk for the protagonist's emotional or communal world.]

<b>[PROMISE LINE — 15–30 words. Explicit comfort-read framing. "Pour a cup of tea." "Curl up by the fire." "Perfect for a rainy afternoon."]</b>

Perfect for fans of <i>Legends & Lattes</i>, <i>Can't Spell Treason Without Tea</i>, and <i>The Very Secret Society of Irregular Witches</i>.
```

## Mobile-first test

Before submitting:
- View on phone — does the above-the-fold (~200 chars) carry the hook?
- View on desktop — does the ~400 char cut land well?
- Paragraph breaks render as blank lines (not smooshed together)?

## Examples of the hook line (study, don't copy)

- "A retired orc mercenary just opened a coffee shop — and it's the most dangerous thing she's ever done." (*Legends & Lattes*, paraphrased)
- "All Emily Wilde wanted was to finish her encyclopedia of faeries. Then one of them started following her home." (constructed in Fawcett's voice)
- "Two women flee the kingdom to open a tea shop. That's the happy part." (*Can't Spell Treason Without Tea*, paraphrased)

## Series blurb (separate from per-book)

Lives on Amazon series page. Auto-pulls Book 1's description by default; customize via Author Central.

```
[WORLD PROMISE — 40–60 words. The inn, the crossroads, the found family, the architectural heart.]

[SERIES SCOPE — 20–30 words. "Six novels, each a standalone story in the same world, best read in order." OR "A growing series of Heartland Fantasy comfort reads..."]

[BINGE PROMISE — 20–30 words. "Perfect for readers who want to sink into a world and stay."]
```

**Write the series blurb at Book 1 launch**, not later. Book 1 readers check the series page on day one.
