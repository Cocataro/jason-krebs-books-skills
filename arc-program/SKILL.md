# ARC Program for Indie Heartland Fantasy

Pre-launch readers who agree to post an honest review in exchange for a free advance copy. The critical launch-week asset: reviews drive Amazon conversion + ad visibility eligibility.

See `references/recruitment.md`, `references/distribution.md`, `references/review-request-templates.md`.

## Why ARCs matter

- **Launch-day reviews** = social proof that drives launch-week buyers
- **15+ reviews on Day 1** = meaningful Amazon conversion lift
- **50+ reviews by T+30** = BookBub Featured Deal eligibility (if not yet accepted)
- **100+ reviews by T+90** = broader ad platform access (BookBub CPM ads perform better with more reviews)
- **Early reviews populate also-boughts** when they're associated with verified purchases

## Target metrics

- **Recruit:** 30+ ARC readers by T-14 (launch minus 2 weeks)
- **Distribute:** All copies sent by T-10 (giving readers 10 days to read ~90k words)
- **Nudge:** Review-ask email at T+3 (after launch day, not before)
- **Aim:** 15+ reviews on launch day, 25+ by T+7, 50+ by T+30
- **Yield rate:** typically 30-50% of ARCs post reviews; recruit 2x your target

## Service comparison (choose one)

### BookSirens
- **Cost:** $10/mo base + $0.10 per ARC delivery ($0.50 per "completed read")
- **Audience:** 60k+ readers, Heartland Fantasy subcategory growing
- **Quality:** decent — readers self-select by genre interest
- **Review rate:** 35-50%
- **When to use:** Book 1 if budget is tight; per-book economics favorable

### Booksprout
- **Cost:** $20/mo flat (unlimited ARC delivery)
- **Audience:** 90k+ readers; Amazon-only reviewers
- **Quality:** higher signal than BookSirens
- **Review rate:** 40-55%
- **When to use:** ongoing ARC program (Book 2+); flat cost scales better

### Reedsy Discovery
- **Cost:** free to list; reviewers pay Reedsy for early access
- **Audience:** higher-quality readers, lower volume
- **Quality:** best editorial signal
- **Review rate:** 25-35% but reviews are more substantive
- **When to use:** launch + ongoing; complement to BookSirens/Booksprout

### NetGalley (Coop)
- **Cost:** $50+/mo via co-op program (shared listing)
- **Audience:** 500k+ professional reviewers, librarians, booksellers
- **Quality:** high but difficult to engage at indie scale
- **When to use:** skip for Book 1; revisit when you have BookBub Deal or traditional interest

### Direct via newsletter (free)
- **Cost:** $0
- **Audience:** your own engaged subscribers
- **Quality:** highest — they already love the voice
- **Yield:** 60-80% review rate
- **When to use:** ALWAYS; recruit from week 4 of pre-launch

## Recommended stack for Book 1

1. **Direct newsletter recruitment** (free, highest-yield)
2. **BookSirens** ($10/mo + per-delivery fees; ~$40 total for Book 1)
3. **Reedsy Discovery** (free listing; supplement)
4. **Launch week:** move to Booksprout for Book 2 onward

Total ARC budget: ~$40-60 for Book 1.

## Timeline

### Week 4 (pre-launch)
- Finalize service choices
- Sign up + complete account setup
- Prepare ARC copy (Advance Reader Copy — watermarked PDF + MOBI + EPUB, marked "ARC - Not for resale")
- Draft recruitment copy for newsletter + BookSirens listing

### Week 5
- Open ARC signups via MailerLite automation to engaged segment + public BookSirens listing
- Target: 30+ ARC commitments by end of week 5

### Week 6
- Close ARC signups if target hit
- Send ARCs to all confirmed readers via BookFunnel or direct email delivery

### Week 7
- Check-in email to ARC readers ("how's the read going?")
- Flag any who haven't started yet

### T-3 to T-0
- Reminder: review on launch day if possible
- Public "thank you to early readers" content (not a hard sell)

### T+3
- Review-request email: soft ask
- Thank early-review-posters publicly

### T+7
- Follow-up to non-reviewers: "any pressure — no pressure"

- Request permission to use pull-quotes on site + social

### T+30
- Recruit top ARC readers for Book 2 ARC program

## Reviewer relationships

The goal is a reader community, not a transaction. Each ARC reader:
- Gets a warm, pen-persona thank-you email within 24h of their review posting
- Is remembered by name in future outreach
- Gets first offer for Book 2 ARC (in ~4 months)
- Is followed on BookTok/Bookstagram if they're creators (supports mutual engagement)

## Do NOT

- Do NOT require a review in exchange for the ARC (FTC violation + Amazon ToS)
- Do NOT incentivize positive reviews ("bonus for 5-star") — Amazon ToS violation, can cause permanent ban
- Do NOT offer free products / gift cards to ARC readers in exchange for reviews
- Do NOT review-swap with other authors (Amazon ToS violation)
- Do NOT post fake reviews or pay for reviews ever
- Do NOT argue with negative ARC reviewers. Ever. Thank them privately if they gave helpful feedback; ignore if they just didn't connect with the book.

All of the above are either illegal or ban-able. Clean ARC program = durable ARC program.

## Handling negative ARC reviews

Not every ARC reader will love the book. Some will leave 3-star or 4-star reviews with legitimate critique. This is fine — it normalizes the review profile (all-5-star looks fake to Amazon + readers). Thank publicly, don't argue. If a review contains factual errors about the book, ONE polite correction is OK; more than one is bad faith.

If an ARC reviewer posts something that breaks pen persona (e.g., "this is clearly AI-generated"), DO NOT engage publicly. Escalate to Board. Apply recovery-playbook.md from ai-fingerprint-audit.

## Success criteria

- 30+ ARCs distributed by T-10
- 15+ launch-day reviews
- 50+ reviews by T+30
- Zero Amazon ToS violations
- Foundation for Book 2+ reviewer pool established
- Pull-quote bank of 5+ great reviews for marketing use (with permission)
