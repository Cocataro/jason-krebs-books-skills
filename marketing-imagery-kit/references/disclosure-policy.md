# AI Disclosure Policy

Per-platform AI-content disclosure rules (April 2026). Verify current requirements before each launch — platforms update policies.

## Amazon KDP

- **Policy:** Since September 2023, Amazon requires disclosure of AI-generated text AND AI-generated images in KDP uploads.
- **Question in upload flow:** "Did you use generative AI in creating this book?" — Yes/No for text, Yes/No for cover/interior images.
- **Board policy:** prefer zero AI in shipped cover → answer "No" on image field truthfully. Text disclosure is a Board-level separate decision (Nicole handles per JAS-3 scope).
- **Penalty for non-disclosure:** potential account suspension.

## Meta (Facebook / Instagram)

- **Policy:** Platform labels AI-generated content automatically; users can also add "Made with AI" label.
- **Paid ads:** Meta requires disclosure if the ad "photorealistically depicts a real person or event" using AI. For atmospheric cozy fantasy mood-shots, generally not required — use label anyway for trust.
- **Organic posts:** Instagram's AI label is recommended when the image is substantially AI-generated.

## TikTok

- **Policy:** TikTok requires creators to label AI-generated content showing "realistic scenes." Cozy fantasy illustrations are typically stylized (not realistic), so technical requirement is softer — but BookTok audience self-polices.
- **Best practice:** label any substantially AI-generated content. Audience trust > platform minimum.

## Pinterest

- **Policy:** Pinterest allows AI-generated content with appropriate labeling. No strict disclosure required but platform may reduce reach of unlabeled AI content.
- **Best practice:** label atmospheric AI pins. Use real cover for product pins.

## Twitter / X / Threads

- **Policy:** No strict platform requirement at this time.
- **Best practice:** if the image is mistake-able for photography, label. Illustration-style AI on a cozy-fantasy Twitter post typically doesn't require labeling.

## Google Ads

- **Policy:** AI-generated ad imagery must be disclosed if it depicts real people or events.
- **Cozy fantasy use:** fictional illustration, typically not subject to disclosure.

## Email / Newsletter

- **Policy:** No regulatory requirement.
- **Best practice:** trust-building. When using AI atmospheric, consider a small footer line ("Atmospheric illustration by [model]") to stay transparent with owned audience.

## Disclosure language templates

### Full disclosure
```
Image atmospherics generated with [Flux.dev / Midjourney / SDXL] from original prompts by Jason Krebs Books. Book cover illustrated by [illustrator name].
```

### Minimal disclosure
```
Atmospheric illustration generated with AI. Cover illustrated by [illustrator name].
```

### Label-only (social platforms)
Use the platform's built-in AI label where available.

## What NOT to do

- Don't claim a real illustrator made something they didn't
- Don't imply the AI image IS the cover
- Don't hide model usage in TOS fine print only — make disclosure visible on image surfaces
- Don't generate AI imagery of Jason Krebs's face and present it as the author photo

## Jurisdiction notes

- **EU Digital Services Act:** generally requires disclosure of AI-generated content in certain contexts. For book marketing atmospherics, follow platform disclosure.
- **US:** no federal AI-disclosure law (as of April 2026); state laws vary; follow platform rules.
- **UK / Australia / Canada:** largely follow platform rules; no unique book-specific requirements.

## Internal audit

Before any marketing asset goes live:
- [ ] Platform AI-label applied where required
- [ ] Disclosure language in caption/metadata if appropriate
- [ ] Image is not fraudulent representation (not claiming human-made when AI)
- [ ] KDP-upload field answered truthfully
- [ ] Illustrator LoRA consent on file (if using LoRA-generated imagery)
