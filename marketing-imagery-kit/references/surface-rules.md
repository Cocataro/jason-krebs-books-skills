# Surface-by-Surface Rules

Expanded rules per marketing surface. Consult before publishing any imagery.

## Amazon product page

### Main cover image
- **Source:** human illustrator only
- **Size:** 2560×1600 ebook; paperback full-wrap PDF; hardcover full-wrap PDF
- **Disclosure:** full KDP AI-content disclosure (since Sept 2023) only if cover contains AI elements — preferred: zero AI elements, zero disclosure

### A+ Content modules
Amazon allows up to 7 modules. Rules per module type:

- **Title banner / logo banner (top):** must match cover aesthetic; cover-illustrated source — human only.
- **Image with text module:** illustrative atmospheric (steaming mug, inn exterior, hearth scene) — AI permitted if style-consistent with cover and passes fingerprint audit.
- **Comparison table:** uses cover thumbnails from the series — human-drawn covers only.
- **Quote module (review pulls):** decorative atmospheric imagery — AI permitted.
- **Series banner:** all cover thumbnails from series — human-drawn covers only.
- **Full image module (large):** atmospheric illustrative — AI permitted if style-consistent.
- **Author bio banner:** photo of author MUST be real; atmospheric background can be illustrated/AI — disclose if applicable.

## Amazon Author Central

- **Author photo:** REAL photo of the author or agreed-upon pen-persona portrait (commissioned human portrait if Jason Krebs has no real face). NEVER AI for this field.
- **Author page banner:** atmospheric OK (AI permitted with style rules); NOT photo-style AI "of Jason Krebs."
- **Bio:** text only, no imagery concern.
- **Additional images:** atmospheric / behind-the-scenes / writing-desk shot. Real preferred; AI atmospheric permitted with disclosure if applicable.

## Social media

### Instagram
- **Posts:** atmospheric AI imagery permitted. Style-consistent with cover.
- **Stories:** same rules as posts.
- **Reels:** moving imagery; AI-generated video acceptable for mood-shots; human content for face-to-camera.
- **AI-labeling:** Instagram has an AI-content label. Use it when the image is substantially AI-generated (>50% of visual content is AI).
- **Frequency rule:** don't 100% AI-saturate the feed. Mix with real photos (bookstack on desk, cup of tea, launch photos) for trust.

### TikTok / BookTok
- **BookTok audience is AGGRESSIVELY against AI covers.** Even atmospheric AI can get flagged if it feels like cover-substitute.
- Use AI sparingly — maybe mood-setting B-roll in a video. Never as a "cover reveal."
- AI-label videos per TikTok's policy when AI is primary content.
- Best-performing BookTok Heartland Fantasy content is face-to-camera readers, book-in-hand, real-setting — not AI.

### Twitter / X / Threads
- **Atmospheric AI permitted** for mood posts, "rainy day reading vibes" type content.
- Cover reveals = cover only, no AI.
- Quote graphics with cover thumbnail = real cover.

### Pinterest
- **Private boards** for moodboard work: full AI permitted (internal).
- **Public promotional boards:** atmospheric AI permitted with disclosure where appropriate; cover pins must use real cover.

## Newsletter / email

### Hero images in email
- Atmospheric AI permitted; style-consistent with cover.
- Don't fake cover presence — if the email is about the book, hero image can be an AI atmospheric OR the real cover.

### Cover reveal emails
- Use real cover. Optionally pair with AI atmospheric scene-setting below.

### "Curl up with a cozy book" atmospheric posts
- AI-generated steaming mug / book-and-blanket / rainy-window scene are fine. This is the sweet spot for AI imagery in Heartland Fantasy.

## Paid advertising

### Amazon Ads (Sponsored Products / Sponsored Brands)
- **Cover-based creatives:** use the real cover. No AI cover substitutes.
- **Lifestyle / atmospheric creatives:** AI permitted with style and brand rules.
- Amazon Ads policy permits atmospheric AI in Sponsored Brands creatives but requires the book cover to be accurate.

### Facebook / Instagram Ads
- Same rules as organic posts.
- Meta has its own AI-disclosure policy for paid ads — apply per platform requirements.

### BookBub ads
- Cover = real.
- Background atmospheric = can be AI.

## Pre-launch / ARC communications

### ARC cover reveal (to reviewers)
- Real cover. Always.

### ARC email / teaser imagery
- Atmospheric AI permitted (mood-building).

### Bookstagram reviewer outreach
- Send real cover + optional atmospheric AI mood-shot that complements.

## Press kits

### Cover asset in press kit
- Real cover, high-resolution, multiple formats.
- No AI mockups.

### Author photo in press kit
- Real.

### Atmospheric / setting imagery in press kit
- Can be AI-generated atmospheric shots with disclosure.
- Labeled as "atmospheric illustration generated by [model]" or similar.

## Merchandise / special editions

### Bookmarks, bookish swag
- Cover art = real.
- Atmospheric designs, pattern prints = AI permitted.

### Special edition printings (if applicable later)
- Cover = real illustration.
- Interior decoration / chapter ornaments / endpapers can be AI-generated (low-consequence surfaces).

## When in doubt

Ask: **"Is this surface a purchase-decision surface?"** If yes (cover, product page, cover reveal, press-kit cover mockup), use real. If no (social mood post, newsletter atmospheric, ad background), AI permitted under the rules.
