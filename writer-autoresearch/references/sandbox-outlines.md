# Sandbox Outlines

Never experiment on Book 1 ship-bound chapters. These are the approved sources for experiment-chapter outlines.

## Source 1 — Book 2–6 warm-up drafts

Books 2–6 already have outlines (on JAS-4, canonical source). Daniel can draft a scene or chapter from a future book as a sandbox experiment.

**Rules:**
- Warm-up draft is NOT a commitment to ship. It's a throwaway.
- If the warm-up happens to land well: it can still ship later (propose to Margaret).
- If the warm-up doesn't land: discarded; lesson learned.
- Only use outlines from Book 2 onward — never Book 1 (too close to ship-bound work).

**Pros:** outline quality matches real production. Best sandbox-to-production generalization.
**Cons:** there are only 5 future books; eventually exhausted.

## Source 2 — Synthetic Heartland Fantasy outlines

Generated outlines matching the Heartland Fantasy rubric but unrelated to The Crossroads Inn series.

**Examples of synthetic outline seeds:**
- "The Honeyhouse" — a retired bee-keeper inherits a magical apiary tangled with a local witch's grudge
- "The Last Library Cat" — an ancient library's black cat is the real curator; a human apprentice arrives unknowing
- "Tea and Treason" — a teashop owner in a capital city hears state secrets in customer chatter; must decide what to do
- "The Bookmobile of the Borderlands" — a traveling librarian in a fantasy frontier world collects + redistributes stories
- "The Farrier's Magic" — a small-town blacksmith discovers iron resonances that amplify simple magic; apprentice arrives

Daniel (or a research subagent) generates a chapter outline to heartland-fantasy-rubric specs (7 beats, cozy stakes, warmth floor, food density, found family). These become sandbox outlines.

**Pros:** unlimited supply; avoids over-fitting to Crossroads voice
**Cons:** voice is inherently different from Crossroads; voice-distance measurements need extra care

## Source 3 — Classic Heartland Fantasy re-imaginings

Take a published Heartland Fantasy book's premise and draft a sandbox chapter in that premise (not copying; reimagining).

Examples:
- "What if Legends & Lattes was set in a tea shop instead of a coffee shop" — draft Ch 1
- "What if The Spellshop was set in a bookshop instead" — draft Ch 1
- "What if Emily Wilde was a mycologist instead of a dryadologist" — draft Ch 1

**Pros:** clear comp-voice reference; premise is proven to work
**Cons:** slight IP sensitivity (re-imaginings, not copies); don't publish these

## Source 4 — Board-suggested outlines

Nicole or Jason (CEO) proposes a sandbox outline for a specific experiment purpose. E.g., "I want to test Daniel's humor register — draft a chapter where Briar is at a harvest festival, being dragged into a dance."

**Pros:** targets specific experimentation needs
**Cons:** introduces Board bias into variant selection

## Per-experiment outline selection

For a single experiment (3 chapters needed):

**Select 3 outlines that span:**
- At least 2 different POV registers (Briar's contemplative vs her wry vs her urgent)
- At least 1 dialogue-heavy chapter and 1 description-heavy chapter
- At least 1 warmth scene and 1 stakes scene

**Avoid:**
- 3 outlines of the same type (skews experiment toward one mood)
- Outlines Daniel has drafted before in a prior experiment (contamination)
- Outlines that are inherently easier or harder than ship-bound chapters

## Outline-difficulty calibration

To ensure sandbox chapters are comparable to ship-bound chapters:
- Each sandbox outline is tagged with a difficulty rating (1–5 based on length + complexity)
- Baseline and variant chapters compared should be on equal-difficulty outlines
- If a variant consistently wins on easy outlines but loses on hard ones: adopt conditionally, flag for production verification

## Avoid contamination

- Don't reuse the same sandbox outline across two experiments (introduces memory bias)
- Don't let sandbox drafts leak into production — they live in `Writer-Experiments/`, never in `Production/`
- Don't use the same sandbox outline for baseline and variant runs (each chapter should be on a distinct outline)

## Seed sandbox outlines (for Board approval)

Initial seed outline set to populate Writer-Experiments/ before first experiment runs:

1. "The Honeyhouse Ch 1" — retired bee-keeper inherits magical apiary (cozy, found-family, warmth floor present)
2. "The Last Library Cat Ch 1" — ancient library, cat as real curator (cozy, magic-through-objects, low stakes)
3. "Tea and Treason Ch 1" — teashop in capital, political intrigue softly woven (cozy + light intrigue; tests stakes calibration)
4. "The Bookmobile Ch 1" — traveling librarian in fantasy frontier (cozy, strong POV, character-first)
5. "The Farrier's Magic Ch 1" — blacksmith with iron-resonance magic, apprentice arrives (cozy + craft focus)

Board approves; Daniel or a sandbox subagent expands each to full chapter outline (2k–3k words of outline per) before use.

## When to rotate

- After 3 experiments on the same outline: retire it (fresh outlines prevent memory)
- When an outline gets adopted as a warmup for a future book: remove from sandbox pool
- Quarterly review: is the sandbox pool fresh? Add 3 new outlines per quarter.

## Cost

- Outline generation: ~$2 per outline in tokens (one-shot research)
- Maintenance: ~30 min quarterly for rotation + freshness check
- Storage: negligible

## Do not

- Do not experiment on Book 1 Chapter 1 (your ship-bound validation chapter) — ever
- Do not publish sandbox chapters as stand-alone short stories without Board approval (IP contamination)
- Do not let Daniel's sandbox chapters leak into the production archive (strict separation)
