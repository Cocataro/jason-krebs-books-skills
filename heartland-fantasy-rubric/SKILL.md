# Heartland Fantasy Rubric

Apply to every outline (EIC greenlight gate) and every completed draft (Dev Editor's structural read, Series Writer's self-audit).

## The 7 Mandatory Beats

A book failing more than 2 of these does not ship without CEO escalation.

1. **Arrival / Threshold** — first 10–15%. Protagonist enters the cozy space (inn, café, cottage, bookshop). The space itself is a promise to the reader.
2. **Found-Family Assembly** — by ~30%. At least two recurring characters named, with tics, and a reason to return.
3. **Small Disruption** — 25–30%. Personal, not apocalyptic. Broken oven, missing recipe, stranger with a secret.
4. **Warmth Midpoint** — mid-book. A scene of collective joy (shared meal, festival, storm-night-indoors). **Non-negotiable.** This is the scene readers screenshot.
5. **Personal Threat** — ~70%. The disruption becomes personal to the protagonist's emotional arc.
6. **Community Response** — ~85%. Found family acts collectively. Protagonist does NOT solo-hero.
7. **Re-Settling** — final 5–10%. Space restored but changed. Door open to next book.

## Stakes Ceiling (genre-defining)

- **Personal** (survival of a character's emotional goal) — yes
- **Relational** (will the friendship survive, will the romance land) — yes
- **Local** (will the inn stay open, will the harvest come in) — yes
- **Communal** (will the town get through the storm) — yes
- **Regional, national, world, cosmic** — **no**. Any stakes-creep above communal is a structural red flag. Flag it, name it, escalate if not revised.

## Warmth Floor

Every 2–3 chapters must contain at least one scene of explicit warmth: kindness shown, food shared, shelter given, comfort offered. Chart it. Any 50-page warmth desert = the book has drifted.

## Food / Sensory Density

- Minimum 1 food-or-drink scene per 3 chapters.
- Specificity over adjectives: "late-autumn stew thickness, root vegetables cut uneven" beats "delicious stew."
- Nomenclature consistency across books (style-sheet anchor).

## Found-Family Beats per Book

Each book earns ≥1 new recurring character to the inn's ensemble. Each recurring character gets one "they showed up for the protagonist" moment per book.

## Romance Curve (for series arc)

Default cozy pacing, Yarros-DNA-inflected:
- Book 1 (~15%): meet / spark
- Book 2 (~40%): trust
- Book 3 (~65%): vulnerability, first meaningful breakthrough
- Book 4–5 (~85%): commitment / consummation (calibrate heat level explicitly in series bible)
- Book 6: challenge-and-recommit

On-page intimacy calibration: Board default is "open-door warm, not explicit." Explicit overrides must be documented in series bible.

## Worldbuilding Discipline

- Lore arrives via teapot, ledger, carved sign — not via exposition dump.
- Magic system: underexplained, ambient. Loose patterns, not strict rules.
- Any paragraph that "explains" the world is a structural red flag.

## Rainy-Day Test (scene-by-scene)

At Dev Edit Read 2, ask of every scene: "Would a sad, tired, anxious reader reach for this on a hard day?" Scenes that fail are flagged in the editorial letter for softening or cutting.

## Humanizing-Fiction Structural Check

Beats landing too precisely on percentage marks is an AI-tell. Manuscripts whose beat map matches this rubric at exact-percentages need deliberate structural irregularity (midpoint at 48%, not 50%; third act that sags before recovering). Over-conformance to the rubric defeats the humanizing-fiction mandate.

## Escalation

If >2 beats fail: revise-or-kill conversation with EIC + Series Writer + CEO Jason. Do not ship.
