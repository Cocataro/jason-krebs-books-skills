# Pre-launch 8-week playbook (indie cozy fantasy, budget-conservative)

## Week 0 — Infrastructure lock (current week)

**Prerequisites:**
- Site live at stayatthecrossroads.com (Landing + Hearth + Book page live — Round 1 of Nina's JAS-34)
- MailerLite account provisioned + signup form wired
- 3-chapter PDF lead magnet (Ben on JAS-38; blocked on Ch 3 shipping)
- Analytics wired (Plausible or Umami — propose to Board week 1)
- GitHub access working

**Deliverables:**
- Pre-launch audience plan (this document is the skeleton; Riley's v1 adds specific tactics)
- 8-week content calendar (16 Ledger posts + 4–5 newsletter campaigns + social cadence)
- Welcome sequence copy (5 emails) drafted for Board review

## Week 1 — Content engine starts, quiet-arrival mode

**Goal:** First Ledger posts indexed by Google; pen persona established across platforms.

- Ship first 2 Ledger posts (Nina writes per Riley's brief)
- Create pen-persona presence on:
  - Reddit: lurker accounts on r/CozyFantasy, r/Fantasy, r/Romancebooks (NO self-promotion for 30 days)
  - BookTok: creator account — decide whose — post 2 atmospheric videos ("the cozy fantasy reading setup," "books next to my tea")
  - Bookstagram: author-style account with atmospheric imagery (coordinate with Sofia for campaign stills)
- Audit competing cozy fantasy author sites (Baldree, Thorne, Fawcett, Klune, Mandanna) for content frequency, cadence, formats
- No paid spend

**Target: 10–30 subscribers from organic discovery + any friends/family seeding**

## Week 2 — Signup acceleration + partnership outreach

- 2 more Ledger posts (total 4 published, SEO compound begins)
- Email 10+ cozy fantasy authors with similar-sized audiences for newsletter swap
- First BookTok content batch: 4 videos (the cozy-reading-nook aesthetic, one pen-persona "about me" atmospheric)
- Optimize landing page signup form based on week 1 data (if signup conversion <3% of visitors, A/B test headline)
- Set up site analytics with Plausible/Umami (propose to Board; ~$9/mo)

**Target: 50–80 subscribers**

## Week 3 — First partnership swap + community engagement

- First newsletter swap lands (with 1 cozy fantasy author — you send theirs, they send yours)
- Reddit lurker accounts begin engaging authentically (not promoting — commenting on others' cozy fantasy threads with helpful adds)
- BookTok: 6 videos this week, experimenting with formats (bookshelf tours, "if you loved Baldree try...", cozy mood reels)
- 2 more Ledger posts
- Begin ARC recruitment planning (research BookSirens vs Booksprout)

**Target: 100–150 subscribers**

## Week 4 — Checkpoint, ARCs open, mid-campaign review

**CHECKPOINT:** If subscribers <100 by end of week 4, escalate to Board. Strategy adjustment required.

- ARC recruitment opens — sign up with chosen service (~$20 setup + $10/mo)
- Post on BookTok: "Looking for ARC readers for cozy fantasy debut — hearth, tea, found family, slow burn"
- Continue swap outreach (target 3 swaps confirmed for weeks 5–7)
- 2 more Ledger posts
- First subscriber survey (short: "which covers do you love? which tropes? what tea do you drink?") — use to inform cover selection + welcome sequence refinement

**Target: 180–250 subscribers**

## Week 5 — ARCs out, welcome sequence tuning

- 30+ ARC readers recruited
- Send ARC copies (first 3 chapters + synopsis — not the whole book until Ch 3 ships and full Book 1 is locked)
- 2 Ledger posts
- Review welcome sequence email performance; iterate subject lines on any <30% open rate
- Begin conversations with Cozy Fantasy Facebook groups for authentic engagement (NOT promo — offering free ARC seats to engaged members)

**Target: 280–350 subscribers**

## Week 6 — Paid ad testing begins (Board-approved)

- Propose paid ad test plan to Board:
  - Meta (Facebook/Instagram): $50 test on lookalike-of-current-subscribers audience, 7-day window
  - Amazon Ads: once KDP page is live (requires Book 1 final cover + upload), start auto-campaign at $5/day
- If Board approves: launch tests; monitor daily
- ARC reviews begin landing; aggregate early feedback (and apply any publishable pull-quote)
- 2 Ledger posts
- Apply for BookBub Featured Deal (6-week application window; 10% acceptance rate; plan on not getting it but apply anyway)

**Target: 380–450 subscribers**

## Week 7 — Launch-week prep, final warm-up

- Pre-load MailerLite campaigns: pre-order nudge (T-3), launch day (T-0), review request (T+3)
- Finalize Amazon Ads launch campaign: keyword list + bid strategy + daily budget
- Pre-load BookTok launch-week videos (cover reveal if not already done, launch-day-sunday-setup reel)
- Queue Reddit posts (genuine, not promotional — "my debut is coming Tuesday in r/CozyFantasy" style is OK in that specific community if pen-persona voice holds)
- Final ARC review push: "Reviews would help on launch day if you can make time"
- 2 Ledger posts

**Target: 450–550 subscribers**

## Week 8 — LAUNCH WEEK

See `launch-week-choreography.md`.

**Target: 500+ engaged subscribers going into launch day**

## Metrics to track all 8 weeks

Weekly scoreboard (Monday):
- Total subscribers (delta WoW)
- Engaged subscribers (opened last 2 emails)
- Open rate on most recent campaign
- Click-through rate
- Traffic sources (top 3)
- Signup conversion rate (visitors → subscribers)
- Content shipped (count)
- BookTok/Reddit engagement (impressions, rate)
- Ad spend (if active) + CPA
- ARC progress (count + reviews committed)

## Contingency: if under-performing

If week 4 subscribers <100, diagnose in priority order:
1. Is Landing page converting visitors? (If <3% → headline/CTA problem, revise with Nina)
2. Is BookTok driving impressions? (If <1k/week → content quality problem, try different formats)
3. Are Reddit efforts getting engagement? (If <50 upvotes/week across community contributions → voice/timing problem)
4. Are organic search impressions landing? (Check Plausible → if <100 uniques/week from search → SEO content problem, revise headlines)

Adjust the top-choked channel first. Don't spread fixes.

If week 6 subscribers <300, escalate paid spend request to Board — Amazon Ads + Meta Ads $50–200 test; likely required to reach 500 by week 8.
