# Post-launch 30-day playbook

Launch-week spike fades within 10 days. The post-launch 30-day window is about converting spike-readers into a retained audience + building Book 2 runway.

## Week 1 post-launch (T+8 to T+14)

### Email cadence
- T+8: post-launch email to engaged list — "thank you, what's next" — shows the Book 2 promise without over-committing on date
- T+10: first short-story Ledger post goes live (coordinate with Daniel + Margaret — small cozy piece, newsletter-early-access by 48h)
- T+14: one-week-in reflection email — what landed, thank community, tease Book 2 outline

### Ongoing operations
- Amazon Ads: optimize keyword targeting based on launch-week performance data
- Review responses: thank reviewers publicly (where appropriate) with warmth, never defensiveness on bad reviews
- BookTok: "one week in" content if organic signal is strong

### Metrics
- Series read-through: Book 1 → Book 2 rate from KDP dashboard (target >40% for the engaged segment)
- Retention: newsletter unsubscribe rate post-launch (target <3%)
- Review velocity: ARC reviews + organic reviews (target 15+ by T+14, 30+ by T+30)

## Week 2 post-launch (T+15 to T+21)

### Focus: retention cadence
- Monthly newsletter format takes shape:
  - One short-form piece (Daniel's Ledger entry OR a curation "cozy reads I'm enjoying")
  - One behind-the-scenes-without-breaking-persona ("what's in Jason's cup this week")
  - Book 2 progress tease ("the inn is still open; the next door is almost ready")
- BookTok: move from launch-focused to "reading life" content to keep pen persona alive between launches
- Begin ARC recruitment for Book 2 (even though Book 2 is ~8 weeks from first-draft complete per studio cadence)

### Paid ads
- Steady-state Amazon Ads budget (whatever Board approved post-launch — likely $5–$10/day continuous)
- Consider Freebooksy or similar promo stack at the Book 1 Countdown Deal slot (plan 30 days ahead for scheduling)

## Week 3 post-launch (T+22 to T+28)

### Focus: review milestone + discovery
- If review count crosses 50, eligible for more ad platforms (BookBub Featured Deal re-apply)
- If review count crosses 100, apply for BookBub Deal
- Start conversations with adjacent Heartland Fantasy authors — "I loved your X, readers who like Y would love Jason Krebs" — natural swap / mutual-mention opportunities

### Content
- Second short-story Ledger post (every 2 weeks cadence from now on)
- BookTok: "one month in" content

## Week 4 post-launch (T+29 to T+30)

### Monthly review
- 30-day sales + KENP aggregate
- 30-day subscriber growth vs. target
- Review count
- Series read-through rate
- Ad spend + effective CPA + ROAS
- Open questions / strategy adjustments for next 30 days

Post a full 30-day report to Board (JAS-3 or dedicated scoreboard issue).

## Retention rhythms after day 30

### Monthly Hearth newsletter
- 2nd Friday of each month
- Jason-voice, warm, short
- One Ledger link, one Book 2 tease, one "what's worth reading" curation
- Keeps the list warm, no hard sell

### Short stories (every 2 weeks)
- Daniel writes between-book short pieces (low-stakes, set in the Crossroads Inn world)
- Newsletter subscribers get 48h early access; public after
- Coordinates with ledger content calendar

### BookTok / social
- Whatever cadence sustainable; 2–4 posts per week is ideal for maintenance
- Continue organic engagement; paid social only at strategic moments

### Amazon Ads always-on
- Sustained $5–$10/day budget after launch
- Adjust keywords monthly based on search term reports
- When Book 2 is within 30 days of release, prepare launch-week choreography again

## Book 1 → Book 2 funnel

Key metric: % of Book 1 readers who become Book 2 readers.

Levers:
- **Series metadata clean.** Book 2 linked in KDP series field. Auto-adds to Amazon "Series Page."
- **Back-matter in Book 1 points to Book 2.** Ben + Nina coordinate (short link on site redirects).
- **Newsletter segmentation.** Tag subscribers who responded to launch-week emails as "warm;" target Book 2 launch separately from cold list.
- **Advance review program.** Top ARC readers from Book 1 get first crack at Book 2 — recruit in week 3 post-launch.

## When to hire a second Marketing person (Phase 3)

- When monthly subscriber growth > 500/mo (list management becomes a job)
- When social volume > 20 posts/week (scheduling becomes a job)
- When paid ad spend > $1000/mo (optimization becomes a job)
- When series reaches Book 3+ with multiple concurrent retention programs

Until then, Riley operates alone with occasional ad-hoc support from Nina on execution.
